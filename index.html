1. Don't be a shit.

If your reasoning focuses on you then you may not be thinking as a team player.

All code gets rewritten at some point.
Don't be the person who is defensive over their code or their views just because it's their code or views. Others may know things you don't, others may have experiences you haven't had, so don't push your view just because it's your view, make sure that others agree it's the right thing to do, and if others disagree then find out why.

2. Give a shit.

Make your team multi-threaded by unblocking others

Start your day seeing if what you're doing is the most efficient thing to get the team moving forwards. Are your colleagues waiting for someone to review their code? then go do it! Is someone struggling with a problem? see if you can help! Even if it's offering to get lunch for someone who's slammed with work it can help. Don't assume that cutting code is the most impactful thing you can do.

3. Don't create unnecessary shit.

Ask yourself; Do I need to build this? Would buying or borrowing make more sense?

A lot have problems already been solved, so, if you're solving a problem ask yourself what will be better than existing solutions? Can you buy or license a solution which will do everything your need? Can you contribute to a project to make it a better fit for the problem you're trying to solve?
